We know how heavy this is

Sound familiar? You're in the right place!

A diagnosis changes everything. Overnight, you're expected to become a case manager, researcher, advocate, and caregiver — with no instruction manual.

The IEP fight

You walk into another meeting unsure of your rights, fighting for services your child is owed.

Insurance and Medicaid mazes

Denials, appeals, waiver waitlists, and prior authorizations. A full job on top of everything else.

The diagnosis journey

Waiting months for evaluations. Trying to understand what a diagnosis means and what comes next.

Finding the right therapies

Speech, OT, ABA, behavioral support. Sorting good help from noise, and getting it covered.

Transition to adulthood

Guardianship, benefits, housing, and work. The cliff at 18 that no one helps you plan for.

The isolation

The feeling that no one around you really gets it, and no clear person to turn to who has been here.

Not only does an advocate understand the path — they walk it with you, side by side.

2

3

Get real guidance

Meet one on one and get practical help. Free where it's offered, affordable where it's paid.
